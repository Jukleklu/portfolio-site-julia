The portfolio issue

Many designers from different backgrounds, industries and levels have something in common: they struggle with the “mighty portfolio”, resulting in feeling constantly lost & insecure about their work, how to properly curate it and what to show.

The possible solution

Customize the experience: whatever design niche creatives are working in, Designer’s Hub will find the matching professional for them. Designer’s Hub is an app that allows to choose from 100+ design experts from all over the world, offering mentorship and guidance to designers and creatives by booking one-to-one sessions wherever they are.

Problem Statement

Users need a way to gain and exchange credible information by/with a real expert because they want to be able to move forward in their design discipline while feeling supported by a trusted source. We will know this to be true when we see a lot of users gathering information and chatting with experts provided by the service of Designer’s Hub.

01 COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS

Having the following three questions in mind, I started identifying my competitors. What are they communicating through their product? What are they missing in their messaging, product, and overall offering? Generally, I found there is a lack of expert & mentoring apps catering specified industry- related needs of creatives. Some promising looking apps, developed for a specific setting, are for internal use in a company or in an university context only - they have been solely designed for a „members only“ community.

Therefore, I looked into two popular coaching apps, “Clarity” and “Growthmentor”.

02 USER RESEARCH

Quantitative Research / Survey

For a Quantitative Research, I started a survey with a total of 20 participants.

90% feel the need to talk about their portfolio and their work on a regular basis

80% are interested in getting feedback from a professional who is specialized and neutral

75% like the idea of scheduling a video session with an expert to talk about their work and/or their portfolio

50% would like a session to be 30-45 minutes long. 30% prefer a longer, 20% prefer a shorter session

Qualitative Research / User Interviews

Based on those findings, I conducted a Qualitative Research via User Interviews, talking to three professional designers from different backgrounds and varying demographics. After analysing the data via affinity mapping, I was able to identify Key Findings & Insights to work with.

03 BUILDING EMPATHY / USER PERSONAS, JOURNEYS & FLOWS

User Personas

To build up empathy und really see through my users' eyes, I worked out several user personas based on my previous findings and analysis. Those personas stayed with me throughout the process to provide a point of reference. I created five relatable personas with different backgrounds, goals and needs to help me walk in their shoes.

User Journeys

After identifying mental models, I looked into detailed User Journeys of Personas Nia (mentoree) and Kirsty (expert) for further iteration to work on the primary tasks of those personas.

User Flows

Looking at those journeys, I was able to further create customized user flows for Nia and Kirsty. Based on those user flows, I was able to start sketching up first ideas and possible solutions.

Usability Testing

With mid to high fidelity prototypes, I conducted a usability testing with 6 participants.

Results & Improvements

Testing results have been analysed via rainbow spreadsheets and affinity mapping, so I was able to make improvements and revisions afterwards.

Usability Testing

A few A/B preference testings have been carried out as well. I used Helio in order to conduct the preference test and recruited from my personal and professional network.

Test Objective

Variation A shows the new logo with Black Font, Variation B has a white typo. All participants have been asked which design they prefer.

Results

The participants preferred option A (70%) over option B (30%). The contrast of a darker typo is higher and therefore will be easier to read. Note: Logo changed again later in the process after conducting another A/B testing
